Estimand–Estimator Alignment in Post-Authorization Safety Analyses

Intercurrent events, clinical questions, and causal interpretation

Joy Nakato

2026-04-13

The problem

In post-authorization safety studies, the question
“Does this drug increase risk of [adverse event]?”
has no single answer.

Intercurrent events complicate the question: - Treatment switching or discontinuation
- Informative monitoring and censoring
- Competing events (e.g., death)
- Loss to follow-up related to health status

Three failure modes: 1. Wrong estimand
2. Right estimand, wrong estimator
3. Right estimand, poor support

Set the framing. The key issue is not modeling choice but defining the question.

Running example

Setting: Antiviral nephrotoxicity

  • Treatment: TDF
  • Comparator: ETV
  • Outcome: renal failure (180 days)

Key complication:
Switching driven by early renal decline → informative

Implication:
Handling switching defines the causal question.

Keep this concrete. Switching is the central complication.

Five estimands, one dataset

Strategy Switching handling Population Interpretation
Treatment-policy Ignored All initiators Real-world effect of initiating treatment
Hypothetical Eliminated All initiators Effect if switching impossible
While-on-treatment Censored Remaining on treatment Effect during exposure
Composite Included in outcome All initiators Failure = event or switch
Principal stratum Restricted Latent subgroup Effect among never-switchers

Each defines a different question.

What decision does each estimand support?

Estimand Use case Decision-maker Limitation
Treatment-policy Real-world safety Regulators, clinicians Includes downstream behavior
Hypothetical Drug toxicity Mechanistic Requires modeling assumptions
While-on-treatment Tolerability window Clinicians Sensitive to censoring
Composite Failure including switching Safety review Conflates biology + behavior
Principal stratum Stable subgroup Methods Not observable

This is the most important new slide. Anchor to decisions.

Same data, different conclusions

  • Treatment-policy → modest increased risk
  • Hypothetical → stronger toxicity signal
  • While-on-treatment → attenuated estimate
  • Composite → dominated by switching
  • Principal stratum → unclear target

The treatment effect is not unique.

Recommended estimands

Primary:
Treatment-policy
→ aligns with real-world decisions

Secondary:
Hypothetical no-switch
→ isolates pharmacologic effect

De-emphasized: - While-on-treatment → informative censoring risk
- Composite → mixes mechanisms
- Principal stratum → not operational

Be explicit. Force a decision.

Why Cox regression may mislead

The Cox hazard ratio:

  • Conditional on survival
  • Depends on changing risk sets

Three issues: 1. Noncollapsibility
2. Selection bias
3. Informative censoring

Result: not a marginal causal effect.

Practical workflow

  1. Define question
  2. Specify intercurrent events
  3. Choose estimand
  4. Map to statistical target
  5. Select estimator
  6. Check support
  7. Sensitivity analysis

Pitfalls: - Defaulting to Cox
- Ignoring switching
- Mixing estimands

Conclusions

  • Estimands define the question
  • Intercurrent events determine interpretation
  • Cox HR often misaligned with safety questions
  • Estimand-aligned methods improve clarity
  • Diagnostics are essential

The key decision is the estimand.
